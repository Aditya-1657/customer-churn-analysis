# 📉 Customer Churn Analysis — Telecom Industry

Exploratory Data Analysis + a Machine Learning model to identify **why customers leave** a telecom company and **predict who is likely to churn next**, so the business can act before they do.

## Dataset
- **Source**: [IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/blastchar/telco-customer-churn) (a widely-used, publicly available industry dataset)
- **Size**: 7,043 customers, 21 columns (demographics, account info, subscribed services, and churn label)

## Objective
1. Understand what customer behaviors and account attributes correlate with churn
2. Quantify churn rate across key segments (contract type, tenure, services, payment method)
3. Build a predictive model that flags at-risk customers
4. Translate findings into actionable retention recommendations

## Tools Used
`Python` · `Pandas` · `NumPy` · `Matplotlib` / `Seaborn` · `Scikit-learn` · `Jupyter Notebook`

## Project Workflow
1. **Data Cleaning** — fixed data types (`TotalCharges` stored as text), handled 11 missing values, removed the non-predictive `customerID` column
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis** — visualized churn rate across contract type, tenure, monthly charges, internet service, and payment method
3. **Feature Engineering** — one-hot encoded categorical variables, scaled numerical features
4. **Modeling** — trained and compared a Logistic Regression and a Random Forest classifier
5. **Insight Extraction** — ranked the top features driving churn using feature importance

## Key Insights
- Overall churn rate is **26.5%** — roughly 1 in 4 customers leave
- **Month-to-month contract customers churn at 42.7%**, vs. just **2.8% for two-year contracts** — contract length is the single strongest retention lever
- **Fiber optic internet customers churn at 41.9%**, nearly double the company average — signals possible service/pricing issues with this segment
- Customers who churn have stayed **18 months on average**, vs. **37.6 months** for retained customers — churn risk is highest in the first year
- Churned customers pay **more per month on average** ($74.44 vs $61.27) — price sensitivity plays a role, especially early in the relationship

## Model Results

| Model | Accuracy | Precision | Recall | F1-score | ROC-AUC |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Logistic Regression | 0.806 | 0.659 | 0.559 | 0.605 | 0.842 |
| Random Forest | 0.761 | 0.534 | 0.783 | 0.635 | **0.843** |

Both models perform comparably on ROC-AUC (~0.84). The Random Forest was tuned with class-balancing to prioritize **recall** — in a churn use case, catching more true churners (even at the cost of a few false alarms) is usually more valuable than a marginally higher accuracy, since the cost of missing an at-risk customer is higher than the cost of a retention offer sent unnecessarily.

**Top factors driving churn** (Random Forest feature importance):
1. Tenure
2. Total charges
3. Two-year contract (protective factor)
4. Monthly charges
5. Fiber optic internet service
6. Electronic check payment method

## Business Recommendations
- **Incentivize longer contracts**: offer discounts to shift month-to-month customers into 1- or 2-year plans
- **Investigate the fiber optic experience**: high churn here suggests a pricing or service-quality gap versus DSL
- **Focus retention efforts on the first 12 months**: this is the highest-risk window for new customers
- **Target the model's high-risk list** with proactive retention offers before contract renewal dates
